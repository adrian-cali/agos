1 · Power Setup

🔌 Your 5V/3A Power Supply → Breadboard

Your 5V/3A supply has plenty of capacity (ESP32 + all sensors together draw ≈ 400–500 mA max). Connect the output terminals to the breadboard power rails.


  • Red/+ wire → Breadboard red (+) power rail
  • Black/− wire → Breadboard blue (−) power rail

The red rail on the breadboard is now 5V. The blue rail is GND (ground).

⚙️ Power the ESP32 from the breadboard

  • Red jumper wire: breadboard 5V(+) rail → ESP32 VIN pin
  • Black jumper wire: breadboard GND(−) rail → ESP32 GND pin

The ESP32 converts 5V → 3.3V internally. With your 3A supply this is very stable — no separate USB cable needed for the ESP32.

🟠 TDS Sensor needs 3.3V (special!)

This is the only sensor that must NOT use the 5V rail. It needs 3.3V from the ESP32.


  • Orange wire: ESP32 3V3 pin → TDS sensor VCC

⚠ Using 5V on the TDS sensor will damage it permanently.

3 · Resistor Placement Guide

R1 + R2a + R2b · Turbidity Signal Divider

The turbidity sensor signal can reach 4.5V but the ESP32's GPIO pins only accept up to 3.3V. Three 10kΩ resistors reduce the voltage safely.

SIG ─── R1(10kΩ) ───●─── GPIO34 │ R2a(10kΩ) │ R2b(10kΩ) │ GND rail

Place R1 in series on SIG wire. The junction between R1 and GPIO34 also connects to R2a. R2a and R2b go end-to-end down to GND. Use 3 separate 10kΩ resistors.

Buy: 3× 10kΩ (Brown-Black-Orange-Gold)

R3 + R4 · JSN-SR04T ECHO Divider

The ECHO pin goes to 5V when detecting a pulse. Two equal 1kΩ resistors split that to 2.5V, which is safe for GPIO18.

ECHO ─── R3(1kΩ) ───●─── GPIO18 │ R4(1kΩ) │ GND rail

R3 goes between ECHO and a breadboard row. That same breadboard row connects to GPIO18 and to R4. R4's other end goes to GND.

Buy: 2× 1kΩ (Brown-Black-Red-Gold)

5 · Important Notes

⚠ TDS Sensor — 3.3V only, never 5V!
Power TDS from the ESP32's 3V3 output pin only. Connecting 5V will permanently damage the sensor.
✅ Your 5V/3A supply is more than enough
ESP32 + all 5 sensors + relay together draw about 400–500 mA peak. Your 3A supply has plenty of headroom — power everything from a single supply via the breadboard rails.
ℹ Bridge the two GND rails on the breadboard
A standard full-size breadboard has two separate GND rows (left and right). They are NOT internally connected. Use a short jumper to bridge them so all components share the same ground.
ℹ pH calibration
Use pH buffer solutions (pH 4 and pH 7) to calibrate. At pH 7, turn the blue trim-pot until the PO pin reads 2.5V. The firmware offset constant is PH_V_OFFSET = 2.5f.
✅ Relay logic in firmware: RELAY_ACTIVE_HIGH = false
GPIO26 set LOW by the firmware = relay energized = pump ON. GPIO26 HIGH = pump OFF.
